Tackling the Monster that is the P2P Lending Industry: How to Build a Lending App in No Time

Okay, let’s get painfully real here— as we grow irritable with the financial institutions of the 21st century, loaning and borrowing money is like helplessly jumping through circus hoops and trapeze bars. Seriously: just imagine yourself as a lender, shooting your best shot at diversifying your financial portfolio through bank investments, only to be slapped hard, center in the face by lackluster interest rates and record highs of the stock market. Ultimately, you saunter into your bank or non-banking financial institution (NBFN) only to be left in confusion by overhead fees and excessive regulation.

Whether you’re an early-phase investor looking to support a rising tech company, or a borrower seeking crucial financial backing from small-large peer investors, the rising waves of the P2P lending/borrowing platforms provide mutual opportunity while hitting two birds with one stone: maximized convenience with minimized short-long run risks.

As referenced in estimates by the PwC consulting company, the P2P lending industry is expected to grow to a mammoth-sized $150 billion by 2025. For emerging businesses, tapping into this expansive multi-billion dollar market sector could prove crucial, as borrowers and lenders are rapidly transitioning to decentralized P2P platforms with digitized infrastructures, convenience, security, and efficiency. More importantly, for businesses looking to gain a large share of this collective P2P lending/borrowing sector, establishing a lending platform creates a sort of ‘win win’ situation for you, the lenders and the borrowers: a low overhead platform with high ROI that supports future creditors and borrowers.

Understandably, this is exciting! Although it seems tempting to nearly dive headfirst into the monstrousness of business registration and lending app development, understanding the nooks and crannies of the P2P lending model is essential in order to define the logistics of your business and the most effective path forward.

The P2P Lending Model

Simply put, the P2P lending model is defined by creditors and borrowers coexisting as equal parties (either individuals or small-large scale companies), where the process of borrowing and lending is not carried out by intermediaries, including credit brokers, banks and traditional financial institutions. Instead, the knot between borrowers and lenders is tied by online P2P platforms that allow the digital transfer (typically via blockchain infrastructure) of funds between the borrower and lender. By effectively removing this ‘middle man’ approach, a more mutual lending mechanism can be developed with complete decentralization and less cost-benefit/risk implications. More importantly, without intermediaries, interests rates are ultimately lower, maximizing the investor’s final profit.

Although P2P platforms are oftentimes glamorized for their scalability and convenience, the mutual benefit comes in with the maintenance of the platform itself. Maintaining a bank is oftentimes an expensive process, involving the rental or purchase of property, staff, specialized software, and computational equipment. Unfortunately, the sum of these overhead fees are oftentimes residually dumped onto the investors or borrowers themselves during the loan process.

Fortunately for you, peer-to-peer lending models are not restricted by banking real estate costs, staff salaries, and sophisticated equipment because they are nearly nonexistent! The result?— most of the profit that would be eaten up by banks and intermediaries are distributed to lenders, borrowers, and the P2P platform. This lending model can be illustrated with the following four steps:

1.) As people engage with your P2P platform, they will register either as a borrower or investor.
2.) Eventually, the borrower submits an application for a loan, specifying both the amount and terms of the loan, as well as brief information about himself/his corresponding company.
3.) The P2P platform identifies any potential risks, determining the credit rating and, finally, adjusting the appropriate interest rate. Oftentimes, these computations can be fulfilled by external people or organized or specialized algorithms and models.
4.) The borrower’s application is forwarded to a large total pool that lenders can freely view, and if loan conditions are suitable, agreements will be made with a particular investor.

Sounds easy enough, right? Although the above-listed functionality of a P2P lending platform ‘works’, designing a platform that distinguishes itself amongst the leaders can prove difficult. More importantly, catering the platform with comfortableness and security in mind, while supporting a vast number of languages, can be a hefty task. Regardless, if you approach the process in a streamlined and organized manner, success is in your business’s favor.

Finally, the Good Stuff: A Crash Course at Building a P2P Lending App…

1.) Choose a Business Registration Form

Before getting your hands dirty in building a P2P platform from the ground up, understanding the nuances of business registration is crucial. As registration largely determines tax reporting, your company’s liability during a bankruptcy, etc, it is essential that a business registration form is decided during the early onset of your P2P platform development journey. The below factors should be considered during this process:

  • Registration as a corporation or LLC (limited liability company) will ultimately protect against creditors/investors in the case of a bankruptcy.
  • Corporations typically demand more requirements and stringency than LLCs, with yearly tax reporting and accounting requirements. Additionally, registered corporations are required to hold shareholder meetings at regular intervals throughout the given investment period.
  • Although different registration forms have different tax statuses, an LLC can ultimately choose to pay taxes as an LLC or as an established corporation.
  • If registered as a corporation, you will be obliged to distribute profits and resulting losses in accordance to any share of ownership defined in the terms between the borrower and investor. With an LLC, distribution occurs in alignment with the operating agreement.
2.) Register a Business Name

Typically, a business name is registered in the state or regional territory in which the business will operate. However, for a peer-to-peer application, this is your main office location at which the platform is hosted and running. When considering your business name, the following should be taken into consideration:

  • The business name should be absolutely new/free. This can be checked on the United States Patent and Trademark Office website
  • Depending on the U.S. state of your P2P headquarters, regulations and registration rules may vary.
  • The name of your company must suggest the platform’s intended purpose or ultimate meaning (e.g. “P2P Lending & Borrowing, Inc” or “P2P Lending & Borrowing, LLC”, etc…)
3.) Domain Registration

Create a short, relevant, and easy-to-read website address that can easily direct user traffic to the main page of your P2P lending application. Consider that...

  • Your domain should be distinguishable and unique. You can easily check the price and availability of your domain through Google or open domain search databases.
  • Register your domain with typically no more than $20 necessary.
4.) Develop your Team

Establishing a robust, diverse, and self-accountable team is crucial as your P2P application moves forward into a potentially large-scale business platform. By tailoring your team with effective staff members, you can streamline the process of growth from a small P2P lending platform into a large-scale app. Consider the following recommendations during team development:

  • Recruit staff members who understand website operation/management, as well as experts in advertising, marketing, finance, and financial law.
  • Include staff with necessary experience in the banking sector who can manually build a credit business and assess/analyze credit risks. Optimizing this portion of your staffing team will be crucial for the integrity of your P2P lending model.
5.) Initial Start-up Capital

During the initial multi-month period of establishing your P2P lending business, you will most likely need funds to issue first loans for initial borrowers . More importantly, collect these funds while being mindful that P2P investors/lenders might not immediately come to your site, depending on your marketing and advertising tools. You can approach collecting initial capital with the following methods:

  • Use ICO (Initial Coin Offering) to attract investments by selling investors a fixed number of new cryptocurrency units (coins/tokens).
  • Attract venture capital by constructing a detailed business plan or pitch deck. With the resulting business proposal, you can reach out to business angels and collect venture capital funding with the expectation that they have an ROI (Return on Investment).
  • Take a bank loan to fund the initial stages of your P2P platform. Fortunately, if your P2P application succeeds and breaks even in profit, you can rapidly return the loan.
6.) P2P App Development

Ultimately, there are several approaches to developing a P2P lending app, especially when integrating blockchain and other decentralized features. You can either create the app from scratch by hiring a blockchain development team or by outsourcing development into services like Trio. Additionally, solutions such as White Label cryptocurrency exchange products to create a customized P2P solution. If you decide to implement White Label, remember the following:

  • White Label sample solutions are not necessarily suitable for unique, novel credit products. Although White Label can integrate into your own credit scenarios seamlessly, the process will require hiring appropriate specialists that can optimize the integration and tailor it for your credit scenario (step 4).
  • Use an open source white label! An open source white label will contain fewer errors and more trustability amongst users.
  • Understand that creating a P2P application from scratch can demand up to 4 to 12 months alone. No fear, however! White Label can break down a year’s worth of development into only three months. Additionally out-of-pocket costs will also decrease.
7.) Create and Design a Web Portal

Your P2P platform’s web portal should be integrated with the max number of payment gateways, have an intuitive interface while supporting several major languages, and have a unique frontpage design. Although White Label is packaged with basic website design templates, you should distinguish your portal with a unique, recognizable, and custom design.

8.) UI/UX Testing & Security

With consideration that the interface and User Experience (UX) of your P2P application will be the first impression for potential borrowers and investors, performing a phase of basic testing and security is crucial.

  • Test user and payment security by ensuring that gateways are encrypted and personal data is masked through several security layers/seals. Additionally , ensure that financial gateways are closed for 1000 and 1 lock. You can create an even more robust security model by having a dedicated website security team that optimizes these encryptions for your app.
  • Perform UI/UX testing by ensuring that users can understand how to register, apply for a loan or invest money. It is better to have a simple and sleek user experience with the same functionality vs. a very complex platform that the average borrower and investor cannot understand!
  • Determine website performance by observing the loading speed of your P2P app on different devices. PageSpeed Insights can be used to determine the cross-platform loadability and speed of your website on devices.
9.) Launch Your App and Create a Technical Support Service

Conduct a marketing campaign that offers first-time users and borrowers discounts, lower interest rates, and more loyal conditions in the loan/investment process. However, remember that scammers run rampant with hackers and competitors that will attempt finding loopholes in your risk assessment and user ID algorithms. After ensuring an efficient app launch with a suitable demographic of initial borrowers/investors, start a technical support service that…

  • Identifies and destroys app errors and bugs.
  • Adds missing features and components to the application that may’ve been overlooked during the time of development.
  • And most importantly, collect feedback from users and understand areas of improvement that augment the user experience.

Key Takeaways…

The emergence of P2P lending and borrowing models in the 21st century has created a multi-billion dollar market sector that business & development teams can tap into early on. More importantly, it has provided an extraordinary opportunity for potential investors and borrowers to exchange funds without the added hassle of supplementary overhead fees and unnecessary regulation. By using our nine-step model for lending app/platform development, you are bound to launch a sustainable P2P model that is more likely to gain support from investors and borrowers at all levels.
